Under-Frequency Response
+300 MW load spike — frequency falls below 50 Hz
A large load connects or a generator trips — demand exceeds supply. Frequency falls. Without inertia, the fall is steep and fast. GFI injects active power from BESS proportional to frequency deviation within 100–200 ms, mimicking synchronous inertia.
1
GFI detects phase angle lagging its internal reference — equivalent to measuring negative RoCoF in real time, without waiting for a frequency filter.
2
Active power injection from BESS (positive MW) arrests the frequency fall — droop and virtual inertia simultaneously.
3
Frequency nadir limited to ~49.7 Hz vs ~48.9 Hz without GFI. Relay trip threshold (typically 49.0 Hz) is never reached.

Over-Frequency Response
−300 MW load drop — frequency rises above 50 Hz
A large load disconnects or excess RE generation ramps in — supply exceeds demand. Frequency rises. Over-frequency is equally dangerous: it triggers turbine over-speed protection and can cause generator trips. GFI absorbs active power into BESS, damping the overshoot.
1
GFI detects phase angle advancing faster than its internal reference — positive RoCoF. It immediately switches to active power absorption mode.
2
BESS charges (negative MW output) at rates up to 300–400 MW/s, absorbing the surplus generation that is pushing frequency up.
3
Frequency peak limited to ~50.25 Hz vs ~51.1 Hz without GFI. Over-frequency relay trips (typically 50.5 Hz) are avoided entirely.

Voltage Support & Black Start
Reactive power — autonomous voltage source
GFI operates as a voltage source behind an impedance — its internal virtual EMF is fixed regardless of grid state. It continuously regulates reactive power to hold terminal voltage. Grid-following inverters trip below ride-through thresholds; GFI stays connected and corrects.
1
During load increase, reactive power demand rises — GFI injects MVAR instantly, voltage nadir held to ~0.96 pu vs ~0.78 pu without GFI.
2
During load drop, brief over-voltage — GFI absorbs reactive power (capacitive to inductive mode switch) keeping voltage at ~1.02 pu vs ~1.11 pu.
3
Black start: GFI energises dead network segments without external reference. Mandated by CEA 2026 for all BESS ≥50 MW.
